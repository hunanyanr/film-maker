# Action — Genre Playbook

Physicality, escalation, kinetic shots, clear stakes. The body in motion.

## Core DNA
- **Spatial clarity.** Viewer must always know where everyone is and what's at stake. Confused geography = dead scene.
- **Escalation.** Each action beat must be bigger or more dangerous than the last. Plateau = boredom.
- **Character under physical pressure.** Action reveals who someone is. Choices under duress define them.
- **Cost.** Violence has consequence. If nothing hurts, nothing matters.

## Act structure
Setup (protagonist's normal skill level + world) → inciting incident (kinetic) → rising action (escalating setpieces, each raising stakes) → midpoint (major reversal — protagonist loses, someone dies, a truth lands) → climax (biggest setpiece, resolution of main conflict) → breath (aftermath, cost counted).

## Pacing
**Propulsive with intentional breath.** Action without breath exhausts. Breathe between setpieces — let a moment of quiet set up the next explosion. Cut fast within action, hold long after.

## Camera
Tracking shots for pursuit. Handheld for intimate combat. Wide for spatial clarity during setpieces. Close-ups for injury/exhaustion. Avoid choppy cutting that obscures action — clarity beats chaos.

## Lighting
Whatever the setting demands, but always motivated. Action in harsh sun feels different than in rain, in neon, in fluorescent. Pick the mood, commit.

## Dialog
Minimal during action. Dry during recovery. Quotable one-liners work when earned.

## Archetypes
- The professional (competent, weary)
- The student/protege (learning, at risk)
- The rival (sometimes ally, often mirror)
- The target/McGuffin (what's being chased, protected, retrieved)

## Common pitfalls
Action without stakes. Invincible protagonist. Villain who monologues. Fight choreography that could be any fight. Forgetting to show consequence.
