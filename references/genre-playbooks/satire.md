# Satire — Genre Playbook

Cultural critique through exaggeration. Makes a point by making it ridiculous.

## Core DNA
- **Target is clear.** Satire needs a real thing it's critiquing — a system, a culture, a type of person. Without a target, satire is just weirdness.
- **Characters commit.** Satirical characters treat absurd premises as normal. The humor comes from their sincerity inside the distortion.
- **Precision, not cruelty.** Punch up, not down. Sharpen against power. Satire that mocks the vulnerable curdles fast.
- **Real stakes.** Characters are at risk of real loss — their dignity, job, future. Without stakes, satire becomes just sketch.

## Act structure
Setup (the satirical world — introduce the distorted premise as matter-of-fact) → escalation (protagonist either rises within the system or tries to navigate it honestly) → midpoint reveal (the rot becomes impossible to ignore) → crisis (the system exerts pressure) → resolution (often bitter, often funny, usually unresolved — the system endures).

## Pacing
**Deadpan.** Let absurd things sit without comment. Don't nudge the viewer to laugh. The world is the joke — the film's tone should be earnest.

## Camera
Often conventional and almost flat — the visual style doesn't wink. Classical coverage, careful composition. Occasional stylized choices to punctuate.

## Lighting
Naturalistic. Satire's humor lives in contrast — ordinary lighting over extraordinary events.

## Dialog
Straight-faced. Characters say outrageous things as if they're obvious. Corporate language, bureaucratic phrasing, social niceties applied to unreasonable situations.

## Archetypes
- The protagonist (often the only honest person, or the naive new arrival)
- The true believer (embraces the satirized system completely — comic or tragic depending on tone)
- The cynic (sees everything, does nothing)
- The authority figure (embodiment of what's being mocked)

## Common pitfalls
Punching down. Meta-commentary that breaks the world (occasional breaking works, constant breaking doesn't). Toothless satire (critiques without targets). Cartoonish targets (too obvious, no insight).
