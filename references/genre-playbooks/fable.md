# Fable — Genre Playbook

Short moral allegory, clean archetypes, childlike clarity. A story that means something without announcing it.

## Core DNA
- **One clean idea.** The whole story exists to land one truth. Don't clutter.
- **Archetypal characters.** The gardener, the bird, the king, the fool. Specificity comes through the execution, not the type.
- **Universal stakes.** Doesn't matter who the audience is — the fable works because the idea is old and shared.
- **Resonance over resolution.** The story ends with an image or feeling that lingers. Not everything needs to tie up.

## Act structure
Compact. Four to six beats total.

1. **The world is.** Establish the rule — what life is like here.
2. **The disturbance.** Something arrives / happens / is noticed.
3. **The relationship.** The disturbance and the character begin to affect each other.
4. **The choice.** The character makes a decision that matters.
5. **The resonance.** The image that sends the viewer out.

## Pacing
**Patient.** Each beat has weight because there are few of them. Hold for the image. Let the quiet work. Fable doesn't explain itself — it shows.

## Camera
Wider than most genres — the fable lives partly in the world, not just the character. Establishing shots that feel timeless. Close-ups for the turning moment. Hold for the final image.

## Lighting
Fable often uses **heightened natural light** — golden, warm, almost mythic. Can go stark and high-contrast for darker fables. The light should feel like it means something.

## Dialog
Sparse. Sometimes none. When a character speaks, it carries weight because it's rare. Voice-over works in fables more than most genres.

## Archetypes
- The keeper (cares for something — a garden, a fire, a memory)
- The seeker (arrives, asks, changes everything)
- The threshold figure (at the edge of worlds — the bird, the stranger, the messenger)
- The old one (has seen it before, speaks in images)

## Common pitfalls
Announcing the moral. Overcomplicating the idea. Making characters specific enough to break archetype (unless intentional). Resolution that closes too cleanly — fable wants an open door.
