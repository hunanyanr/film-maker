# Drama — Genre Playbook

Character-driven, grounded, emotional truth. Internal conflict is the engine. The stakes feel enormous because the characters care.

## Core DNA
- **Small stakes, big feelings.** One conversation can be more devastating than a car chase.
- **Subtext over text.** What characters don't say matters more than what they do.
- **Ambivalence.** Nobody is fully right or fully wrong. Both sides have a case.
- **Change is the point.** By the end, someone has been altered — not defeated, altered.

## Act structure
Classic three-act: setup (who these people are + what's fragile), escalation (pressure that forces choices), resolution (the choice lands, consequences ripple).

## Pacing
**Held beats.** Long takes on faces. Silences where the viewer can hear what isn't being said. When drama feels like it's rushing, it's failing.

## Camera
Medium shots dominate — you want to see two people in the emotional geography of a room. Push-ins during revelations. Rack focus between characters. Locked shots for weight.

## Lighting
Naturalistic. Window light, lamp light, honest sunlight. Heightened drama gets dramatic light; grounded drama stays real.

## Dialog
Characters talk around things. Interruptions. Unfinished sentences. The line that lands hardest is usually the simplest one.

## Archetypes
- The person trying to hold it together
- The person who sees through the performance
- The absent party (ex, parent, child) shaping decisions without being in frame
- The catalyst (new arrival, old letter, news of death) that forces movement

## Common pitfalls
Over-explaining emotions. Making every conflict resolve cleanly. Dialog that tells instead of shows. Letting the story announce its themes.
