# Thriller — Genre Playbook

Rising tension, pressure-tested protagonist, twist. Plot-driven with character stakes.

## Core DNA
- **Clock is ticking.** Something will happen if the protagonist doesn't act.
- **Trust breakdown.** Allies are suspect. Information is incomplete.
- **Escalation.** Every scene raises stakes — more danger, more at stake, fewer options.
- **The twist reconfigures everything.** A late-story reveal that reframes what came before.

## Act structure
Setup (ordinary world, threat glimpsed) → escalation (threat engages, protagonist pressured into action) → midpoint reversal (what they thought they knew is wrong) → climax (direct confrontation, high stakes, compressed time) → resolution (pyrrhic victory or clean win, rarely both).

## Pacing
**Propulsive.** Shorter beats. Hard cuts during action. Held beats only for the deliberate moments of pressure (the silence before the knock). Never let the viewer get comfortable.

## Camera
Handheld in chase/action. Tracking for pursuit. Close-ups on faces during pressure. Rack focus to reveal threats. Wide shots to show vulnerability in a space.

## Lighting
High contrast. Deep shadows where threats hide. Practical lights giving small pools of safety. Avoid flat lighting — thriller needs visual danger.

## Dialog
Terse. Functional in action scenes, loaded with subtext in quiet scenes. Characters lie and we know it.

## Archetypes
- The protagonist under pressure (ordinary person thrown in, or professional compromised)
- The unreliable ally
- The hidden antagonist (not revealed until late)
- The civilian/bystander whose safety raises stakes

## Common pitfalls
Twist viewers see coming from the trailer. Too many reversals (stops meaning anything). Protagonist too competent (no tension). Hollow stakes (character survival without emotional stakes).
