# Romance — Genre Playbook

Relationship arc, vulnerability, shifting power. Two people changing each other.

## Core DNA
- **Mutual change.** Both characters affect each other. A romance where only one person transforms is a character study.
- **Obstacle is internal.** External forces (distance, circumstance) are flavor; the real obstacle is something in the characters.
- **Vulnerability as stakes.** The risk of being truly seen. The cost of truly seeing.
- **Moments over plot.** Romance lives in a shared glance, a hand touching a hand, a line said softly. Plot exists to create those moments.

## Act structure
Meet (distinctive, specific to the characters — not generic) → complication (obstacle that would be easy to overcome if they were different people) → separation or crisis (the obstacle wins, briefly) → choice (one or both characters change enough to choose each other) → resolution (earned, not given).

## Pacing
**Alternating intimacy and distance.** Close-ups of shared moments. Wides of the world pulling them apart. Held beats during silent agreements. Romance needs breath — don't rush through the feelings.

## Camera
Close-ups dominate emotional moments. Two-shots for connection. Singles when they're apart emotionally even in the same room. Push-ins during realization beats.

## Lighting
Warm for love scenes, cool for disconnection. Golden hour is a cliché because it works — use consciously. Candlelight for intimacy. Natural light for tenderness.

## Dialog
Specific. Not movie-romantic — person-specific. They talk about the weather, their day, a thing they noticed. The love is in how they listen, not in what either says.

## Archetypes
- The vulnerable one (wants to be seen, afraid of being hurt)
- The guarded one (has walls, is the story about lowering them)
- The catalyst (brings them together — friend, job, accident)
- The obstacle (ex, parent, own belief system)

## Common pitfalls
Miscommunication plots (both sides could fix with one conversation). Grand gestures without earned intimacy. Love-at-first-sight without character work. "Fixing" a broken character through love (fraught — handle carefully).
